What Was Carried, What Was Lost Chapter 13

 I landed on top of a young man’s back, my legs soon submerged in the water as he sprinted in. Others chased after him, but immediately gave up once the water reached my lower back.

 “I’m going to let go of your legs. Once I do that, put your arms around my neck, alright? Try to keep your brother around your neck too,” The man said as we went deeper and deeper.

 “Okay,” I said, but my voice quavered. I didn’t notice that I had been crying until now.

 As soon as I replied, he let go of my legs and I felt my body drifting in the open sea, my arms wrapped tightly around the man’s neck.

 “Kick under the water with me.”

 I suddenly felt the force of his kicks as he started to swim forward, and I nearly got torn off his neck. But I hung on, kicking my feet with him. So this was the strength of an adult.

As if on cue, a wave that would have pushed me back to the shore loomed ahead of us, but the man just told me to tighten my grip and to hold my breath. So I did, and when the wave crashed into us, I felt my fingers slipping. But immediately after we hit it, we made it out the other side, the man still propelling us towards the boat.

The gunshots immediately resumed, and shouts were heard from behind us.

 “Don’t look back,” The man whispered between his heavy breaths.

 Still, I snuck a glance behind me, my body tensing at the sight. The soldiers finally made it through the mountain of rocks, and were grabbing the people like they did back at my house—but more violent, tearing them down and beating them with a large black baton. Some tried to flee, but were then shot in the back. The man let out a grunt.

 “Hey. Eyes forward.” He said in frustration. But his voice wavered.

There was nobody in the water now, with only two dead corpses floating with the current toward the shore. The water around us was stained red, the reek of blood and death overtaking the salty ocean and filling my senses. Yeomjae squirmed relentlessly between me and the man and screamed out in discomfort. The boat was not so far away now, but his screams alerted the soldiers on shore. Bullets rained towards us.

The man cursed loudly as he warned me to hold my breath. He then plunged under the water’s surface, swimming as fast and as deep as he could as bullets pierced into the water, trying to hide from the soldiers’ view. But after a few seconds, we had to go back up for a breath, and then go down again. I didn’t even have time to wipe the water from my eyes to get a good view when we were above the water’s surface before we plunged back under. It was a constant pattern, and after diving underwater for around five more times, the bullets stopped as we got closer to the boat.

When I thought that it was never going to end, I suddenly felt familiar hands haul me out of the icy water and onto a hard surface. The boat. As soon as my feet touched the wood, I slipped and fell onto the floor after a burning sensation erupted on the bottom of my feet. My shoes were gone—my feet were red and raw, soaked in blood, skin peeling. I gritted my teeth, groaning in misery. To try to ignore it, I looked up and saw familiar faces look down at me. Halmeoni, Eonni, Taejae, Oppa, and…Abeoji. My eyes widened when I saw the face of my father, and I would have jumped into his arms if my limbs were not completely given out. It had almost been a year since I last saw him. He looked slimmer than before, but his wide smile still looked the same. I also noticed unfamiliar faces on the boat, but I ignored them. The man who brought me to the boat was slumped on the floor.

Tears welled up in my eyes. I tried to wipe them away, but ended up dragging salt into my eyes instead.

Our moment of reunion was cut short when bullets lashed towards us again. I screamed as everyone ducked low against the floor, and Oppa immediately started to row with the help of a different man.

However, the gunfire was slowing. A bullet whizzed past my head as I curled up on the ground, covering my ears. One struck a stranger that was on the boat with us and knocked her overboard, then another. The boat was too crowded; we could barely move.

THUNK

The boat rocked as we all looked at the floor. Water rushed in.

Everyone rushed to find something to plug the bullet hole, but found nothing. My small pouch of my belongings was gone, and so were everyone else's. Panic seized us, and we all rushed to scoop the water out, which, by the way, did absolutely nothing.

 “Here!” Oppa’s voice rang amidst the panic with his shirt crumpled up in his hands. He then stuffed it into the hole, “This might not do much but keep scooping out the water!”

The water was up to our calves now as we scooped the water out with our hands. Everyone worked relentlessly as Oppa rowed even harder with the same stranger. The bullets were now all missing the boat, splashing into the water around us instead. It was as if something was protecting us, but I couldn’t think much of it.

But right as it started, everything stopped. The bullets ceased, and silence blanketed us. We all froze, the stillness strange to our ears. We were too far from the shore for the inmingun to aim.

The shirt that Oppa had stuffed into the hole was holding well enough, but we all knew it wouldn’t last long.

I looked up to see if everyone was still safely on the boat. Eonni covered both Taejae and Yeomjae with her body while Halmeoni was curled up beside her. Abeoji was helping a wounded man. Two more strangers were also on the boat. The man who had saved me was gone.

Blood dripped from our bodies, staining the oak wood. The waves rolled gently against the hull. Rays of sunlight spilled across the water, a cool breeze kissing our skin.

It felt cruel that the world could look so beautiful while we bled inside a broken boat.

And yet it was strangely comforting too.

It meant we were still alive.

But something felt missing. The boat was too still. As we all looked around us, my stomach dropped, and for a moment we couldn’t move or speak as we stared at the empty bench.

Oppa was missing.
