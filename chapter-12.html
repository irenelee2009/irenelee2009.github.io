What Was Carried, What Was Lost Chapter 12

 The light from the ship extinguished as multiple guns fired into the air. Eonni grabbed me by the back of my shirt and shoved me down to the rocks, pain sparking through my limbs. I couldn’t see anything as I felt my body rolling and tumbling on the ground as Eonni dragged me somewhere. Where was Halmeoni? Taejae? Yeomjae? I couldn’t feel him on my back anymore.

Eonni screamed something to me, but I couldn’t understand what she was saying. I opened my mouth to tell her to stop, but before I could say anything, ice swallowed me whole.

It took me a second before I realized that I was thrust into the ravaging ocean. I thrashed under the violent currents as I tried to regain my balance, but the waves pushed back, throwing me around like a rag.

I suddenly felt my arms graze something hard and gritty. Land. I immediately thrust my hands out, clawing my way out of the frenzy of waves, coughing seawater from my lungs. I crawled my way out, my body trembling as I touched the cool sand. My limbs felt heavy. Looking up, I would have screamed aloud if I had the energy to do so. Rushing down the mountain was a moving mass which was unmistakably a group of soldiers—a patrol—with rifles raised. There were more voices around us. Strangers. Looking around, I finally noticed a group of people—around twelve—racing towards our boat. Who were they?

Climbing onto my feet, I quickly surveyed my surroundings, my head finally clear and ringing with urgency. I was on sand. The rocks stood in between me and the mountain where the soldiers were now coming down from, their endless gunshots waking the whole village far away. Suddenly, I felt a hand grab my arm. I shrieked and tried to push away, but Halmeoni’s voice shouted to stop.

 “You need to swim, Hwajae. Swim to the boat,” She said, shaking me roughly to regain my senses.

 “Who are these people? Where–”

 “I saw them. They’re defectors who were hiding in a cave,” Halmeoni said, “They must have seen the boat.”

 “What about you?” I asked, flinching as a gunshot, too close, rang in the air.

 “I’ll swim just fine. But I need you to swim with Yeomjae.”

 I looked at Yeomjae who was on Halmeoni’s back and nodded, “I will.”

 “You must hurry. The sun is rising.”

 I nodded again as I took Yeomjae and tied him onto my back. He was wide awake and had his arms tightly around my neck.

 Halmeoni then started running as fast as she could towards the water. The sight was heartbreaking, but I didn’t spare a moment to feel...anything really. Rushing into the water, I stretched my arms and legs to fight against the current. I was able to clearly see the boat now. I was also able to see the other people in the water swimming towards the boat.

The gunshots kept coming, and one person in the water screamed as he leaked red, his head drooping under the water’s surface. But I didn’t stop fighting against the current. Soon, my feet lost contact with the ocean floor, and a shiver went down my spine, my toes grazing nothing.

The ocean felt like it was trying to tear me apart, its waves constantly slamming me under. I panicked, fearing that Yeomjae would get lost. I didn’t know where Halmeoni was anymore as I soon was pushed under the surface more often than actually getting closer to the boat. My whole body felt like it was on the brink of collapse.

As soon as I broke the surface to take a deep breath, a giant wave suddenly crashed down on me, sending me deep down into the abyss. I blubbered as the waves felt more formidable under the surface, shoving me backwards as I reached behind my back to grab onto Yeomjae, who also clung to me with his nails painfully digging into my skin. I tumbled and tumbled, sand burning against my skin, my ears ringing, my head nearly bursting from nausea as I finally felt my head slam onto land, my neck nearly cracking on impact. My head finally reached the air as I gasped, throwing up. My whole body racked with pain as I sprawled onto the sand, my bones splitting.

I still clung to Yeomjae who was trembling on my back. The soldiers were nowhere to be seen but the gunshots still filled the air. I wobbled to my feet and stared at the boat that was now…bigger. No—not bigger.

Closer.

I gasped as I could now see the figures on the boat. Why was the boat getting closer? It was already dangerous, and getting closer would give the soldiers an advantage. I squinted my eyes as I searched for Oppa.

There.

At the very edge of the boat, I saw him waving his arms frantically at me. I couldn’t see the details of his appearance, but the shape of his body was what I recognized. Was he moving the boat closer for me? Quickly looking around, I realized that the rest of my family was nowhere to be seen. They must be on the boat already, since Eonni had guided Taejae and Halmeoni through the waters. I was the only one left.

I couldn't swim to the boat. Even if it got closer, I still couldn’t fight past the waves. It was too strong, and I was too small. Hell, I was almost the same height as Taejae—how could they expect me to do this all on my own? It looked like Oppa understood, but still waved for me to try again. We didn’t have time, he seemed to say.

I choked as I took another step towards the ocean, my bones screaming from the pain. I had to have broken a few of them, but the adrenaline that blinded my senses seemed to have blanketed most of the pain. For now, at least.

Wobbling, I began to run towards the water again, but I felt the wrap around my back loosen, and before I could react, the knot unwinded, and I felt Yeomjae fall off my back. Shouting in panic, I halted to a stop, my feet getting caught in the sand, making my body tumble to the ground. I hissed in pain. Spinning around, I saw Yeomjae sprawled on the ground, screaming aloud. I frantically crawled back to him, my knees burning on the gritty sand as a bullet pelted into the sand a foot away from me.

Grabbing him, I swiftly re-tied Yeomjae on my back and slicked my hair back with my hands, my legs shaking as I pushed myself back up onto my feet. I wasted too much time. I balled my fists and sprinted as fast as I could into the waves. Gunshots still rained down into the water as four people were swimming towards the boat. I lunged into the water, my whole body fighting against the current. Sea water immediately filled my ears and nose, burning my eyes like they were on fire.

As I lifted my head from the water’s surface to take a breath, my stomach dropped when I saw another large wave surging towards us. It wasn’t big for adults, for they were easily cruising through it. But to me?

Before I could make a decision, the wave folded on top of us, dragging me deep underwater. Again, another episode of tumbling and rolling nearly knocked me out as I gasped for breath, soon regaining my senses on the sweet sandy shore.

This was impossible.

I had now endangered not my own life but my whole family because I just couldn’t swim. But then, I froze and listened. The only sound I could hear now was the crashing of waves against the shore. Even the large trees on the mountain had stopped rustling their leaves as if they were…hiding. An eerie silence had blanketed us, but there was something small still ringing in the air. Not gunshots, but the metallic clicks of boots hitting stone. Many of them.

Too close.

 It was them. I jumped up from the ground and ran towards the waves once more. But as soon as I took a step forward, my knees buckled. No, no, no. My body couldn’t betray me now. Not yet.

I tried to pull myself up, but my arms gave out and I laid on the ground, breathing hard.

Move.

Maybe if I fell asleep now, I wouldn’t feel it when they found me.

Move.

Yeomjae was squirming on my back, as if sensing the danger.

Move.

At least he had some energy left.

Move.

With the side of my face flat on the ground, I stared at my right hand that was beside my face. There was enough light in the sky now that I could see clearly, and that was when I realized that my fingernails were all torn off, bleeding and bruised. I was too tired to even notice the pain.

But something else was missing. What was it? I furrowed my brows, forcing my heavy eyes to stay open. Something was missing.

My mother’s rings.

My eyes widened, my jaw slackening. The rings were gone, my bare fingers twitching awkwardly, noticing the heavy weight of them gone. I never noticed.

My throat choked and tears threatened to form in my eyes. She was lost—lost in the relentless sea or in the crevices of the stones, I did not know. It felt like she passed away all over again. The panicked voices of other defectors near me started to drown away, my eyes slowly starting to close for the last time. But suddenly, a familiar voice pierced over the others.

 “The girl!” Oppa shouted. “The girl and the baby!”

I looked up. He pointed straight at me.

“Bring them to the boat and you can come with us!”

The people on shore with me froze. It was as if the focus of the whole world had now zoomed onto me and Yeomjae, their stares boring down on us.

Move.

Suddenly, five strangers who were on the shore started running towards me, their arms outstretched. I immediately sat up and twisted Yeomjae around to my chest, hugging him tightly as desperate hands yanked me up.
