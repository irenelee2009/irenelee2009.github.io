What Was Carried, What Was Lost Chapter 11

 By the time we reached the village, the sky had turned dark, shadows pooling between the houses as we slipped through the narrow dirt streets. Looking around, I saw people packing up their belongings from their stalls, their hushed voices blending into a low murmur in the salty evening air. Dim lanterns hung from the arms of merchants as they walked by, illuminating the paths. I stuck close to everyone else, my arms nearly brushing against Eonni’s, but I still felt a creeping sensation itching against my skin. We saw only one soldier, slumped against the wooden wall of a house, asleep.

Soon, the end of the village was far behind us, and darkness and the wild sounds of nature surrounded us.

The ‘sea of rocks’ was just like how I envisioned it. It stretched on endlessly, its rough edges jutting into the sky in uneven piles. To our left, the rocks met the shoreline where they dropped into a low cliff into the dark water. To our right, however, a huge mountain loomed—the kind none of us wanted to enter in the dead of night. There was no way around it.

The night sky cast a dim light. I looked around and watched everyone’s expression shift from disbelief to utter defeat.

 “Halmeoni, will you be okay?” Eonni asked her before I got the chance. I watched her sag with a sigh but she nodded as a response. Maybe if we hadn’t walked the unnecessary distance, Halmeoni would have more strength. I bit my bottom lip in frustration.

 She began to step over the first rock at the edge, her feet landing on another with uneven edges, causing her body to rock slightly. But she turned around and gave us a thumbs up.

 Following after her, I carefully stepped onto a rock and made my way to the next, my feet cramping from the instability. The faint light cast by the partially covered moon was barely enough for us to navigate through the rocks. Taejae, with Yeomjae on his back, was not far behind me, following the same path that I took. In front, Eonni was beside Halmeoni.

The rocks were big enough so that it was dangerous to leap over to the next, but too small to rest on comfortably. So instead, I hauled myself onto the rocks on all fours, my nails scraping against the salt-crusted stone. I sometimes bumped my shins and knees against the edges.

 “This is exhausting,” Taejae mumbled from behind me, “Can’t we stop just for a moment?”

 Looking back I nearly gasped at the sight. His arms and legs were bruised, some parts of his clothes soiled. His face was smudged with dirt. Yeomjae thankfully was safe on his back, but even the wrap looked like it was gathering sweat and grime. I nearly stumbled back after examining my own arms. They were bruised up as well, with my nails throbbing from irritation and salt. I never noticed until now, and all of a sudden a sharp pins-and-needles sensation flared throughout my body. My back was slick with sweat, and my hair stuck to my face whenever the cool wind disturbed it. Taejae stared at me as if he was having the same realization as me.

“Yeomjae’s fine. Can you take over? I’m getting tired.” He said.

“Of course.” I reached over and gently unwrapped Yeomjae, holding him in the process. Then, I turned around and allowed Taejae to wrap the baby around my back, and the familiar heavy weight pressed down on my back. Looking forward, I saw Halmeoni and Eonni watching us from afar. I opened my mouth to speak, but Eonni shook her head, waved her index finger in the air, and waved for us to catch up.

After what felt like hours, my fingers were numb and the bottom of my feet throbbed. Yeomjae was still sleeping soundly on my back while I crawled over each rock one by one. When I spared a glance behind me, the village was dark and silent. At least we were making good progress. Soon the silence was disturbed when Taejae tapped me on my arm when I took a second to rest.

 “Look over there!” Taejae gently whispered from beside me, “Doesn’t that look like a small boat?” He was pointing towards the sea.

I squinted my eyes but couldn’t see a thing in the pitch darkness. “What are you talking about?”

 “Do you not see it?”

 I caught sight of what Taejae was talking about. It was a silhouette that looked unmistakably like a boat. My eyes widened. For a moment I wondered if exhaustion had made me imagine it. But Eonni and Halmeoni had also stopped, staring.

The boat was small, with nothing but one little sail that was barely noticeable. It was still a good distance away from the shore, the large, strong waves rocking it side to side. I climbed as fast as I could through the rocks and made it to the edge of the cliff, next to the rest.

 “That has to be theirs, right?” Eonni murmured,

 “Yes. It has to be,” Halmeoni then reached inside her cloth pouch and withdrew a small metal disc. Wait.

 “A mirror,” Eonni replied before I could realize what it was, “Where did you get that?”

 “I brought it with me from home.”

 Halmeoni lifted the mirror toward the sky. After a few tense moments, a thin blade of light flickered across the water. My breath hitched as I stared at the boat to catch some kind of sign.

But, for what seemed like hours, all I saw was darkness. Was everyone asleep? With a sigh of defeat, I dropped onto the ground, my joints groaning. Taejae followed my lead, and soon, everyone was sitting down.

My head dipped down every few seconds, my eyes fluttering with exhaustion. How long were we to wait? Was this not Oppa’s ship that we presumed it to be?

I was about to close my eyes when I saw a flicker of light. Jerking awake, I pointed with shock as I saw a small little flame on the ship. It was very small—barely visible—but it had to be a sign. Then, it grew and grew until it was bright like a small beacon. It looked like a star.

I almost cried with relief, my shoulders sagging and my neck loosening.

The sea was all I could hear now, its waves crashing against the shore.

We were so close.

We would swim to the boat, slip into the darkness, and by morning—

 CRACK

 My smile immediately dropped once I recognized the sound piercing the still night air.

A gunshot. Then a shout.
