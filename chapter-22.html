What Was Carried, What Was Lost Chapter 22

When we arrived at the beach, we started asking where the new man was to the defectors who lived there.

"Don't have a clue," One defector shrugged, "I did hear that he was wandering somewhere along the beach. He could have moved inland, but I have no idea."

We thanked him and moved on to a different defector, one who lived a bit farther away. She lived in a small tent with two small sons and her husband.

"I heard that some villagers gave him some food because he looked to be near the brink of death," The woman explained as she fed her children something I didn't even want to look at, "You should look around the shore. He wouldn't have the energy yet to go inland."

We thanked her, and decided to do just that.

Walking at a hurried pace along the coast, we watched for any sign of Oppa, though we didn't know what to expect when we saw him. All the descriptions about the new defector were grave. Abeoji took the lead in front of us, but noticing how his hands were shaking, he looked...scared. I couldn't blame him.

After walking for what felt like hours, we were sure that we had almost made a loop around the whole island. The sun was already starting to set. We decided to ask a man who sat alone by a tree. He wore a tattered shirt and faded pants, and his hair reached his chin. He was just sitting there, staring out into the ocean while mumbling to himself.

"Keep walkin'. He jus'a bih 'way from'ere." His words were slurred, and I realized by the red flush on his cheeks that he was drunk. Really drunk. Eight\...empty bottles were next to him. "I dunno why you all're searchin' for that crazy man. You folks're defectors like me! You have nothin'...we all have nothin'. We're not gonna survive...we're not gonna survive." He wailed, weeping into his arms. I looked at Eonni with wide eyes, and she just shrugged her shoulders and shook her head. Abeoji calmly asked him once more and he pointed in the direction of the defector, still weeping. So we left the man weeping at the tree and continued onward. His wails were still heard even after he was out of view. At this point, a rock seemed to be lodged in my throat. We were so close.

As we walked faster, we soon caught sight of a lone figure limping towards the ocean. It must be him. The 'crazy' defector. He was struggling as he entered the waves, which was odd. The water was already up to his waist, but he kept going. My stomach dropped. Was he trying to drown himself? I couldn't recognize him from afar, but he definitely didn't look like Oppa. Defeat consumed me.

"Is that..." Taejae murmured, but didn't complete his sentence. Nobody responded, but suddenly, Abeoji started running. I shouted in surprise and started running after him.

The man was now chest deep in the water. He wasn't trying to keep himself afloat, nor did he want to return to the shore. My legs screamed on each impact on the sand, but I ignored it. Abeoji kept on running, so I followed. He seemed to know something I did not. It had been a long time since I last ran like this—or tried to at least.

As we got closer, my eyes seemed to water and my breathing became heavy. I struggled to run through the sand as I tried to make out who the person was. The figure looked familiar, yet so strange at the same time. The man didn't seem to see us yet as he kept walking into the waves. The closer we got, the tighter the knot in my chest became. It had to be him. But we couldn't say anything—as if our emotions and thoughts were swallowing our words, unable to process what was going on. Suddenly, Abeoji let out a shout.

"Chunjae!" He yelled as we ran towards the man. It was as if saying his name had broken a spell. The man stopped and turned to us, and Halmeoni burst into tears. I stopped running, staring at the man. It was him.

Oppa's body seemed to tremble as everyone ran into the water—everyone except me. I stood where I stopped, just staring. For months, we didn't see him. For months, we thought he was dead. For months, he was out there alone, trying to survive and catch up to us. He was alive.

As we got close enough for Abeoji to outstretch his arms to embrace him, Oppa gave us an exhausted smile and immediately tumbled into his arms, his body going limp.
