What Was Carried, What Was Lost Chapter 14

We only saw the quiet ocean waves stained red. A few bodies floated around the boat, but none were Oppa.

We all called his name, our breaking voices growing louder. I gripped the boat’s ledge until my knuckles turned white, gritting my teeth so hard that my jaw ached. As soon as we reunited and became a family again, the inmingun ripped us apart.

When would they stop?

 “He’s gone,” a stranger whispered.

 “He needs to be somewhere,” Halmeoni argued back, “We can’t just leave him,”

 “We have to,” The stranger shook his head, “Or the inmingun will come after us.”

 We all stared at the ocean.

 “Look!” Taejae pointed towards the shore, and we all spun to follow his gaze, expecting to see Oppa. Instead, we all stared in horror. On the beach, the inmingun were dragging three small boats toward the water.

 “We need to get out, now!” Abeoji jumped onto the bench and rowed as fast as he could away from the shore. If we rounded the corner of the mainland before they got on their boats, we would be out of their sight.

Rowing as fast as he could, we all sped away from the shore. Abeoji’s face darkened with each stroke of the oars while he never tore his eyes away from the waters. Compared to the stranger who rowed with him, his pulls were not as strong, as if he was trying to slow the boat. I studied each wrinkle and change of color on his face, looking for a hint to what he was thinking. But nothing gave way and so I inevitably gave up, looking away when Abeoji finally noticed my stares.

I held my breath, shielding myself by staying low to the floor. My heart seemed to beat in my head. With every pull of the oars, however, we soon were too far for the soldiers to catch up.

Once we were out of view, we all slumped to the floor, our eyes staring blankly in front of us.

 “We’re heading for the islands off the coast,” Abeoji finally said, but his voice sounded deaf to my ears. My head still throbbed, and my stomach churned from all the sea water that I had swallowed earlier. The echoes of bullets still rang.

As the blood dried under the sun, I sat in silence, staring at the glittering ocean, expecting him to surface beside the boat, shaking seawater from his hair.

Oppa was missing, and we were all leaving him.
