What Was Carried, What Was Lost Chapter 10

 Once we finally got to the village, we noted that it seemed busier than the other day. The merchants were plentiful, and there were more soldiers on guard.

Halmeoni, Taejae, and Yeomjae were all washed up from the river, and we looked as good as new. We looked like a normal family passing through.

The dark blue waves lapped against the wooden ports that stretched out from the coast. Soldiers stood beside every port, monitoring each ship that came and went, even the ones passing by.

 “This is going to be difficult,” I muttered under my breath as I glanced at the soldiers loitering between the ocean and the village. Our meeting place wasn’t necessarily at the village, but we had to walk through it to get there, and Oppa’s boat would have to pass by to pick us up.

 “Stop staring,” Eonni jabbed me discreetly with her elbow, “You look suspicious.”

 I jabbed her back but stopped looking at the soldiers. We couldn’t get caught now, not when we were so close.

Walking through the bustling streets was easy enough. Travelers from towns all over North Korea had come here for trade. Though this wasn’t a large village, its goods were plentiful. The shouts from the merchants drummed in my ears as we walked past stall by stall. Two girls ran through the tangled streets, laughing as if it was some sort of fun game that they came up with. One brushed past me, and I felt the disturbed air rush by me. Turning around, I stared at them with longing as they looked barely older than me. Funny how the lives of people right next to you could be so imaginably different.

 “Noona,” Taejae tugged at my sleeve and I followed his gaze. A large man seemed to saunter towards, his gait carefree and unbothered but his eyes saying otherwise. They were fixed on me. My heartbeat quickened as I met his gaze, and before I could warn everyone else, the man knocked his arm roughly against my shoulder, forcing me to jerk around. At the same time, I felt something being shoved into my pants pocket. I lifted my hand to see what he put in there, but he merely shook his head as he also turned around from the force, facing me as he walked away backwards.

 “Keep walking. That ain’t for the street.” He called out in a brusque tone, waving his hand dismissively at me. My hand stopped midair from entering my pocket as I narrowed my eyes. How odd. Before he completely turned around to walk away, he winked. Winked. I nearly gagged as I turned around to continue down the street. Glancing around, I saw a few merchants and bystanders watching me. Whatever it was, now was not the time.

 “What was that for?” Halmeoni furrowed her brows as she faced me.

 I shrugged. “Let’s keep going.”

 Eonni was staring at me with suspicion, but I ignored her. Perhaps I’ll look at what the man put inside my pocket once we reached our location.

Leaving the village was easy. Finding the exact meeting place was difficult. Unraveling the weathered letter from Oppa, we analyzed its content, rereading his directions over and over again. The meeting area was at a small cove, its shores hidden from view by the base of a rocky mountain that jutted out of the land. But the shores stretched as far as the eye could see, and the base of a rocky mountain was nowhere to be seen.

“This has to be the wrong village,” Eonni crossed her arms as Halmeoni reread the letter to herself.

“That was the right village,” Halmeoni shook her head as she nodded her head towards the village that was now far behind us. “Chunjae must have written this letter far in advance, visiting the location before actually setting off into the islands.”

“We’ve already walked so far. It must be nearby,” I furrowed my brows as I squinted against the bright sunlight that rained down on us.

Our feet ached from walking for so long as we continued onward. The waves lapped against the shore, the cool ocean breeze gently wafting past us. As we walked, I watched Taejae as he ran to the shoreline every so often to get chased back by the waves.

We finally found the location, the small mountain jutting out of the forest and into the sand right beside us. Behind it was, like Oppa stated in his letter, a little dip into the land that created a little safe pocket from peering eyes. When we got there, we sat under the trees, hidden in the shade. That was when we could finally rest, waiting for the boat. Sighing as I lay on my back, I still felt the eerie feeling in my guts. This all felt so…easy.

“Okay, I’m not stupid, Hwajae. What did the man put in your pocket?” Eonni finally asked, her eyes roaming around my pants pocket.

“Oh! I totally forgot about that.” I quickly sat up and dug my hand into my pocket, drawing out the small piece of folded paper.

Unfolding it, I nearly gasped when I recognized Oppa’s handwriting.

“It’s from Chunjae?” Halmeoni darted from her place and sat close next to me, peering into the letter.

Dear all,

I hope your journey has been in your favor so far, but I have unfortunate news to tell you. If you are reading this letter, you must already be at the safe spot. However, I regret to tell you that there have been some slight alterations to the plan. We cannot move the boat past the village to the safe spot due to complications. We are hidden not far away from the village, but it is the opposite way from where you departed. You must return to the village and pass through again, and to the actual safe spot where you must cross large rocks that might be difficult to pass. Once you cross the sea of rocks, you will find us on the boat at night.

Chunjae

Silence blanketed us as we reread the letter once more.

 I groaned with frustration. “You’ve got to be joking.”

 “Don’t blame it on him,” Taejae shot me a look, “It’s your fault for not reading the letter sooner.”

 I turned towards him, annoyed, “Well it’s too late now.”

 Halmeoni sighed as she picked herself up onto her feet, with Eonni helping her. She was tired because of me.

 How could I be so stupid? We now have to walk all the way back to the village, and pass a ‘sea of rocks’. Oh, could the day get any better?

The sun was already sinking. I sighed, my shoulders dropping in exhaustion.
