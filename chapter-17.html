What Was Carried, What Was Lost Chapter 17

 As we rowed south, we discovered another island which Halmeoni claimed to be Changnyeong-do. She said that on maps this island was right below Seommaeup. Changnyeong-do was not much bigger than the previous island, but it had more trees and greenery.

 “There might be more defectors living there.” Abeoji said as he steered the boat closer to the shore. The island was quiet; I didn’t even hear any birds nor a rustling of trees by the wind. It was eerie, but I’d rather be on an eerie island than stay in the ocean on a small boat.

As soon as we took a step onto the sand, I saw movement within the trees. We all froze when we saw a man emerge. He was tall with broad shoulders and arms. He looked strong, but I could tell that something was wrong, noticing the deep hollows in his cheeks. It was a common sight nowadays, but I couldn’t help but remember how Yeomjae had similar cheeks.

 “Who are you?” A man narrowed his eyes. He kept his distance, but his voice rang loud and clear, “I don’t believe you are the inmingun seeing how you have women and children with you.”

 “This is my family. We left North Korea and came from Seommaeup.” Abeoji explained, “We come in peace and in need of rest.”

The man looked us up and down for a moment, “Alright. Follow me.”

 The man turned around and beckoned us to follow. After dragging our boat deep into the forest, we made our way towards their own hideout, which was similar to Seommaeup, but maybe even worse.

People didn’t look like they had much food themselves.

Instead of tents, the defectors had dug two deep, long pits in the ground where they slept in, separated by men and women with their children. In the large pits, the dirt ground was layered with grass and leaves to make it more comfortable. It was something I have never seen before. I frowned at the sight of it; it must be very uncomfortable. Why not sleep in tents?

I tried not to stare at the people but I couldn’t help but notice how thin they were. Their skin was gray and pale, and there was no lively chatter. Everyone only whispered, their voices cracking and airy. Nobody had silver utensils or pots lying around. There wasn’t even the crackle of a fire.

 “You came from Seommaeup, right?” The man asked and we nodded, “Then you must know about the food issue. It happened to us too, but much earlier. I’m sorry, but we won’t be of much help if you are hungry.”

 I exchanged worried glances with Eonni, but it was Abeoji who spoke first.

 “It’s quite alright. We don’t plan to stay here for long.” He said, giving the man a reassuring smile.

 “Really?” The man furrowed his brows, “Where are you off to?”

 “South Korea.” Abeoji answered with a curt nod, “We can’t waste more time, so we will be leaving after a good night’s rest.”

 “Alright, I’ll let the others know,” The man said and pointed to a small plot of grass above ground, “You can rest there for the night.”

 “Thank you,” Abeoji said. Everyone quickly walked to the grassy area and immediately collapsed on top of it. Thankfully, the grass was dry and soft, and I didn’t need to sleep in a long hole full of strangers. Lying down, I closed my eyes. The warmth of the sun against my face felt like a gentle kiss, and I almost dozed off when I felt a phantom chill brush against my back. Without thinking, I sat up and counted. Four. Where was the fifth? I jumped up, panicked, and almost said his name out loud when I remembered. Oh. Right.

Halmeoni looked at me as if I grew another head, but I smiled weakly and sat back down.

We were still lying in the same place when the sky became dark. Halmeoni and Abeoji had already fallen asleep, but the rest of us couldn’t.

“Tell me a story about Eomeoni.” Taejae asked Eonni. We all sat in a small huddle, whispering to each other.

“I’ve already told you everything about her.” Eonni sighed, “I don’t have the energy to repeat the stories.”

Though I couldn’t completely make out Taejae’s face in the darkness, I could tell by the silence that he was glaring at Eonni. Before anyone could speak, a grumble interrupted us. We all looked at each other and grinned sadly. There was nothing we could do about our hunger.

“I have an idea,” Eonni whispered to us, “Let’s go to the shore.”

The defectors were fast asleep, so we had no problem going out, and the crashing waves muffled any noises we made as well. Taejae and I sat on the sand as we watched Eonni wade into the dark waters, looking as if she was searching for something. After a moment of silence, she plunged her hands into the water and drew something out of it, cupping it in her hands.

“I saw these when we came ashore,” Eonni said as she came to us, holding out her hand. We took a peek inside and gasped. Inside her cupped hands was a small crab, no bigger than a pebble. “It’s not much, but I think it’ll do.”

 Taejae immediately bolted towards the water, his pants soaking through instantly. I hesitated, looking at the dark waters glimmering in the moonlight, but followed.

Eonni set up a small fire while we searched for crabs, and soon we gathered a handful of them.

I sat by the small fire, as I watched Eonni spear the crabs and dangle it above the flames. As small droplets of their juices and seawater dripped into the fire and sizzled, a tiny yet present aroma of cooking crab wafted in the air. It made my stomach growl more aggressively. When they turned a color we thought was right, we all took one and nibbled on it. It was mostly its shell, and barely any meat inside. I frowned with disappointment, but I still gnawed on the shells until the hunger in my stomach settled a bit.

When we ate all the crabs, the fire had died out already. But the fire wasn’t needed anymore as the sky was turning brighter by the minute. I watched Taejae as he was still chewing his final crab when Eonni’s eyes widened as she pointed behind me—toward the ocean. Turning around, I stared as the sky was turning an orange hue, painting the waves in gold. I sighed, staring at the sight.

I hoped Yeomjae was seeing the same thing I was.
