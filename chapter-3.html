What Was Carried, What Was Lost Chapter 3

A few weeks later, Oppa left in the dead of night, when all the nearby houses were silent and dark. We made sure nobody was watching when he slipped quietly out the back door. No gunshots followed, no shouts—only silence, so we assumed he was safe.

He didn't apologize either.

As if he expected it.

Before he left, he gave Halmeoni a pat on the back while having a tired, almost warm smile on his face. Some may say it\'s an act of regret and encouragement, but in my eyes it didn't look like it. I took deep, shuddering breaths as I watched. I almost screamed at him, the only thing preventing me being Eonni, who silently stood beside me, with sad eyes and clenched fists.

He told us that he must go back to the islands to search for our father. After that, he would come back for us, to escape to the islands together. It sounded too good to be true.

I lay on my mattress, staring up at the dark ceiling, listening to the lone owl outside and the soft snores of Taejae who slept beside me. We once roamed around the village carefree and greeted passerbys with a smile; now we flinched at every shout beyond our gate.

Oppa was gone. The one who kept us laughing all throughout the night. The one who kept us in line if we made a mistake. The one who would put us first when he was clearly falling behind.

I knew that Oppa would never return.
