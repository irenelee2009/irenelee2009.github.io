What Was Carried, What Was Lost Chapter 8

After crossing the second mountain, we finally saw the small village port, the vast ocean stretching behind it. We arrived with hungry stomachs as we had eaten the small amount of food we packed from home. We hoped the village market would have food to buy.

However, we couldn't just walk into the village, for it would stir suspicion. We were all weather-worn, our bodies dirty. It would look too obvious that we were not normal travellers passing by through the main roads.

We sat at the top of the small mountain that looked over the village. Watching the bustling streets, I counted the soldiers that were keeping watch.

"There's around five soldiers on guard duty," I said as I broke the silence, "I don't see any sign of Oppa either."

"We still have time," Halmeoni murmured behind me, "If Chunjae is still accurately following his letter, we still have a day left until his boat will be near the coast. Until then, we must recover as much as possible."

Silence returned, but a heavy blanket weighed our shoulders. It was as if we all knew the important question without saying it out loud. But it was Eonni who started speaking.

"How much money do we have?"

"Not much," Halmeoni answered before we could speak, "I believe it will be enough to buy some food for all of us, but that would be it. Unless we sell our belongings..."

"That's too risky," I shook my head, "The soldiers will definitely notice and question us. Besides, what do we have to sell? They are all old trinkets."

Taejae turned to me, "We could sell your rings."

What? Is he out of his mind? My eyes widened, but quickly turned to boiling anger. "Sell?" I looked down at the gold rings on my fingers.

"It's real gold! We can trade it for food."

"Are you crazy?" I leapt up from the ground with my fingers curled into the palms of my hands, "These are all we have left of Eomeoni. I will never sell them. Ever."

I shot a glare at Taejae, opening my mouth to say more, but Eonni caught it and shook her head from beside him. Sighing in frustration, I decided to become quite interested in the grass below me.

The sun was pleasant today, its warm rays shining gently across the coast. The salty smell of the ocean breeze was not so overwhelming as well, and I thoroughly enjoyed the moment of peace we had on top of the mountain. That is, until Eonni got up from where she sat, quieting the singing birds that were perched on top of tree branches.

"I will go down to the village in search of food," She announced, looking determined, "I'll go alone. It'll be safer that way."

"What?" Halmeoni furrowed her brows, "You can't do that. It's too dangerous."

"Let me come with you." I suggested. Eonni's eyes widened as she frowned.

"No. You need to take care of Halmeoni, Taejae, and Yeomjae."

"Halmeoni can take care of them." I turned to her for confirmation. She hesitated for a moment, but then nodded.

Eonni looked at us both. "You got to be kidding me."

When she knew that she couldn't talk her way out of it, she sighed.

"Fine. But I'm gonna do all the talking. Are we clear?"

I shrugged, "Clear."

My shoes were brittle after hiking through the rain-flooded paths on the mountain for days. The sun had dried the caked mud on it, and my legs were stained with dirt as well. We had to first get cleaned up before we entered the village, and half the time we looked for a stream on the mountain.

The trees rustled in the wind as we combed through rows and rows of trees, stopping periodically to listen for running water. Instead, we only heard the distant shouts and noises from the village below. My arms had started to bake into a slightly red hue, the top of my head hot to the touch.

"There has to be a stream," Eonni furrowed her brow as she examined my arm, "Any longer in the sun and you'll get burnt."

She lightly flicked my arm which made me hiss in pain, glaring at her when she grinned. We were quite far from where Halmeoni and the others were, but we made a few markers to follow back if we ever got lost.

After what felt like hours, Eonni finally heard running water after moving a bit down the mountain. Hauling myself across a few more obstacles, I finally beheld what Eonni was so excited about. A large rushing river knitted around trees, its clear water so cold that my skin burned when I dipped my foot inside in excitement.

I unwrapped the cloth pouch that hung limp around my waist and laid its contents on a large flat rock next to the river. It had one more pair of clothes all rolled up in a tight bundle, a bit weather-worn from the rain but clean enough. Eonni had done the same. With all the excitement of finally having the chance to clean ourselves, Eonni and I had completely forgotten our initial plan for the moment.

Peeling off our dirty clothes, we jumped into the water with the clothes fisted in our hands. The water rose to the top of our stomachs in the deepest part, and there were soft pebbles below that were pleasant to walk on. Eonni had completely submerged her body. With the dirty clothes in my hands, I shook them in the water till it was clean, the dirt pieces following down the stream. When I was done, I laid them on top of another large rock, the sun bathing its surface. Then, jumping back into the water, I swam back and forth until my skin was sleek and shiny.

Eonni looked better than ever as color returned to her cheeks and her eyes glowed with satisfaction. "I can't remember the last time I ever felt this clean."

"Me neither," I answered as I chased minnows, "I wish we could stay here forever."

"That's silly. Why would you want to stay in a river forever?" she laughed and squeezed the water out of her hair.

"I obviously didn't mean in the river," I rolled my eyes as I watched Eonni step out of the water.

"Hm," she mused, her back towards me, "Get out and dry yourself. We need to get going."

Sometimes her authoritative entitlement annoyed me.
